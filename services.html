Frequently Asked Questions

Questions clients
actually ask.

How do you handle projects where requirements aren't fully defined yet? +
This is actually the norm, not the exception. My Discovery phase is specifically designed for this situation — the needs analysis and action mapping process helps define requirements collaboratively. For projects where requirements will likely evolve during development, I'll recommend a SAM (Successive Approximation Model) approach: rapid prototype, stakeholder feedback, iterate. This prevents the classic situation of a 40-slide course being rejected because the brief was misunderstood.
Do you work with a client's existing Storyline templates or brand guidelines? +
Yes — this is the most common working arrangement. I'll work within your existing templates, brand guidelines, and component library. If I identify accessibility issues with the existing template (which is common), I'll flag them and recommend fixes — but the decision to address them is yours. What I won't do is build new courses that inherit those issues without documenting them explicitly.
What's your policy on rush projects? +
I can accommodate compressed timelines with advance notice. Rush work means abbreviating some phases of the process — typically prototype review and iterative feedback cycles — which increases the risk of rework. I'll always tell you honestly what's achievable in a given timeframe and what we'd need to cut to get there. What I won't do is promise a timeline that requires skipping QA or accessibility testing.
Do you provide ongoing LMS administration or is it project-by-project? +
Both. Most engagements are project-based with a 30-day post-launch support period included. For clients who need ongoing LMS administration (Mindtickle, Docebo, SCORM Cloud), catalogue management, and regular course updates, retainer arrangements are available. These work well for organisations with a continuous pipeline of training content who need a dedicated L&D resource without a full-time hire.
Can you work with our subject matter experts who aren't familiar with eLearning? +
Yes — this is the standard situation. I prepare SMEs with a brief onboarding document that explains their role in the process, what to look for during reviews, and what they don't need to worry about. The storyboard is written in plain language so technical SMEs can focus on content accuracy without needing to understand instructional design principles. I manage the translation of their expertise into effective learning design — that's my job, not theirs.
What does accessibility compliance actually cost in time and budget? +
When built in from the start (the correct approach), accessibility adds approximately 15–20% to development time on a standard Storyline project. When retrofitted to a finished course (the expensive approach), it typically costs 40–60% of the original development time plus the risk of requiring structural changes. The calculation is straightforward: build it right the first time. All my Full Development projects include WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as standard — it's not an add-on.
