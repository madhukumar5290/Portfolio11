Module 01 of 02
What is SCORM?

SCORM — Sharable Content Object Reference Model — is the technical standard that allows eLearning content to communicate with a Learning Management System. When you publish a Storyline or Captivate course to SCORM, you're creating a package that any SCORM-compliant LMS can launch, track, and report on.

SCORM tells the LMS three things: did the learner launch the course, did they complete it, and did they pass the assessment. Everything else — where they clicked, how long they paused, which branches they chose — is not captured.

SCORM 1.2 and SCORM 2004 are the two versions you'll encounter. SCORM 1.2 is older but almost universally supported across LMS platforms. SCORM 2004 adds sequencing and navigation rules but has inconsistent LMS support. In practice, most organisations default to SCORM 1.2 unless they have a specific reason to use 2004.

The LMS-SCORM communication happens through a JavaScript API exposed by the LMS in the browser window. The content calls LMSInitialize() on launch and LMSSetValue() to send completion and score data back to the LMS for storage against the learner record.

Module 02 of 02
xAPI vs SCORM — The Difference That Matters

xAPI (Experience API, also known as Tin Can) was designed to fix what SCORM couldn't do. While SCORM is limited to tracking course completions inside an LMS, xAPI can track any learning experience — a conversation, a job task, a simulation, a mobile activity — and send that data to a Learning Record Store anywhere, not just an LMS.

The xAPI statement format is beautifully simple: Actor → Verb → Object. "Madhu completed Module 1." "Guest User answered Question 3 correctly." Every learner interaction becomes structured, queryable data.

The LRS (Learning Record Store) receives, stores, and queries xAPI statements. Unlike an LMS, it doesn't deliver content — it stores the evidence of learning. You can connect an LRS to Power BI, Tableau, or any analytics platform to build dashboards that show what learners actually did, not just whether they clicked through a course.

Use xAPI over SCORM when you need more than pass/fail data, when learning happens outside the LMS, when you want to connect learning data to performance data, or when you're building a serious analytics capability. Otherwise, SCORM 1.2 remains the practical, universally-supported default.
